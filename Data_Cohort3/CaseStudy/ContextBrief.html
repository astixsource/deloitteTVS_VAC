Country Overview

Krakozhia – Background and Demographics

Krakozhia, officially the ‘Republic of Krakozhia’, is a country in the South western part of Asia. It is a federal republic governed under a parliamentary system, consists of 18 states and 5 union territories and its capital is Krazville. The population consists of close to 80 percent Muslims. Their native language is Krakish and the culture is based on their Muslim religion which is observed strongly across the nation.

The country has a vast landmass, including extensive mountainous areas with the majority of the 22 million population inhabiting the flatter areas. Their currency is called the Krakozhian Dollars (KD). In 2020, Krakozhia emerged as one of the world's fastest-growing economies.

Key challenges

Despite having a favorable demographic profile, labor and skill shortage is one of the key concerns for the Krakozhian industry. A survey administered across leading EPC (Engineering, Procurement and Construction) companies in the country indicated that they are unable to find adequate number of skilled workers for running their operations. Companies have also reported that they are facing the brunt of this shortage in the form of slippages in meeting confirmed orders. Also, there is an upward pressure on wages that is now pervasive.

The country struggles with a number of significant barriers that continue to hamper the development of urban infrastructure: Corruption, complex leadership structures, poor infrastructure, capability gaps (due to illiteracy and insufficient skills development) and fiscal deficits are a drag on the development. Krakozhia needs to address the current problems of developing good infrastructure, solid waste disposal, flood management, storm water, sewage and drainage system etc. resulting in urban decay, traffic gridlock and thereby a deteriorating quality of life for many of its citizens.

The wave of urbanization that is sweeping across Krakozhia represents one of the greatest opportunities as well as one of its most serious challenges. The country requires a highly efficient and sustainable solution for servicing urban areas and can reap the benefits by using technology from other parts of the world.

Current Political scenario

Krakozhia underwent an election in the year 2016 and the citizens chose the new Government with the hope of reviving and reinvigorating the country. The elected Government stood true to their promises and in the last four years, Krakozhia has taken major strides towards development in a short span of time and hence got re-elected in 2021.

A new round of elections is due in 2026 as the current regime completes its tenure. The Government has identified building smart cities — world-class, self-sustainable habitats as a key imperative to win over the confidence of Krakozhians once again and get re-elected. Hence, the effective execution and timely completion of the ‘IRIS City’ Project is a key, high priority agenda for the political realm.

The Smart City Project
Pioneering the Krakozhian growth story

The Smart City way

According to the United Nations, the proportion of urban population in Krakozhia is expected to increase dramatically to the point that by 2050, almost 70% of its population will become urban. Left unchanged, rising populations and the subsequent impact on energy resources is creating an unsustainable future for the country.

In such a context, the Smart City concept has appeared as a new paradigm of intelligent urban development and as an important factor to shape sustainable socio-economic growth. A Smart City can be referred to as a planned, coordinated and interconnected digital system of citizens, transports, communications, services, infrastructure and utilities.

Pillars of a smart city

Social Infrastructure: Components that work towards developing the human and social capital, such as education, healthcare, entertainment, etc.

Physical Infrastructure: Cost-efficient and intelligent physical infrastructure such as the urban mobility system, housing stock, energy system, etc., integrated through Information & Communications Technologies (ICT).

Technological/ Institutional Infrastructure: Activities that relate to governance, planning and management of a city using ICT.

Economic Infrastructure: Components that attract investments and generate employment opportunities e.g. Incubation centers, Skill Development Centers, Industrial Parks, IT / BT Parks, Financial and Trade Centers, etc.

The 'IRIS City' Project
Developing the Ideal Residential & Industrial Smart City

The Krakozhian government is developing the ‘Krazville Industrial Corridor’ (KIC) as a global manufacturing and investment destination. Within the KIC Corridor, ten investment nodes have been identified which are proposed to be self-sustained smart cities with world-class infrastructure, road and rail connectivity, domestic/ international air connectivity, reliable power and other quality social infrastructure, and provide a globally competitive environment conducive for setting up businesses.

Figure 1: Location of the Site

Amongst the various investment nodes identified, the one which has been identified as a flagship project, is the development of ‘IRIS City’ (Ideal Residential and Industrial Smart City) and its development was kick-started in 2019.

The site for the proposed smart city has a total area of 100 square kilometers. It is planned adjacent to the already developed capital city of Krazville and hence enjoys the availability of existing and proposed transportation networks and physical infrastructure. The land for the delineated site is already acquired and the location of the site along the city of Krazville is given in Figure 1.

The project aims to attract skilled human resource and generate direct employment for about 58,000 people.

The master-plan for the city is approved, land has been acquired and partners have been chosen by the Government of Krakozhia.

It is planned as the first built environment showcasing the application of future city planning strategies in the country.

Project implementation structure

The project is proposed to be executed in two phases:

Project Implementation - Phase 1

  • The Government of Krakozhia and the KIC Corporation have formed a ‘Joint Venture Company’ (JVC), to provide for land and trunk level infrastructure including:
  • Roads – National highways, Internal roads: 50% streets with dedicated cycling tracks, Pedestrian friendly walkways
  • Water supply and waste water recycling
  • Power: Development of an on-site Captive Power Plant backed by a Grid-Based Power Backup for 24x7 Power Supply and Smart meters
  • Underground utilities: Ducting, underground cabling
  • Connectivity and Technology: Wi-Fi hotspots and kiosks, Energy efficient lighting with multi-functional poles, Intelligent street lighting, Smart traffic signal management, Smart metering - water and electricity
  • Site level landscape infrastructure (Green spaces)
  • The shareholding of both the equity holders of the JVC is 50% each. The role of JVC is highly critical for successful execution of the project as it helps in co-ordination with all other government agencies.
  • The Stage 1 is projected to complete in FY 2024 (five year duration – FY 2019 to FY 2024).
  • ‘The Evergreen Group of Companies’ has been appointed by the JVC to carry out design and execution of the trunk level infrastructure as a ‘Design-Build’ (DB) contract.
  • After the completion of trunk infrastructure, the operation and maintenance would be carried out by an entity appointed by JVC by transparent bidding process.

Project Implementation - Phase 2

The project implementation phase 2 is planned to be initiated from 2023.

Smart IRIS 1.0

  • The phase 2 is projected to complete in 2028 i.e. FY 2023 to FY 2028.
  • The major project in this phase is the institutionalization of transport system for the IRIS City
  • Also, this phase will include creation of signature buildings for residential use, commercial purposes, incubation centers, etc.
  • The successful implementation of this phase will act as a primer to attract industries to the IRIS City and facilitate the demand amplification for consecutive phases.
  • Post this, the JVC shall monetize the unutilized development potential to recover cost of land & trunk level infrastructure. It will also explore future growth and development opportunities for the Smart City.

The Evergreen Group of Companies

The Evergreen Group of Companies (‘EGC’ or ‘The Company’) is a global company engaged in a multitude of services ranging from technology, engineering, construction, manufacturing and automation services. Headquartered in Mumbai, India, the Company’s major businesses include:

  • ‘Evergreen Construction and Infrastructure’, amongst the world’s top contractors having expertise in – Transport infrastructure, bridges, ports and metro rail;
  • ‘Evergreen Realty’, real-estate development arm of the Evergreen group
  • ‘Evergreen Power’, which provides concept-to-commissioning solutions for power plants and power transmission and Distribution networks
  • ‘Evergreen Shipbuilding’, which offers concept to design for new ship builds, as well as repair and retrofit spanning both defence and commercial vessels
  • ‘Evergreen TechnoBiz’, which delivers a range of technologies, services & automation solutions across transportation, industrial products, telecom and hi-tech and process industry

Even though Evergreen has been involved in the development of smart cities in the recent past, the current project is the largest engagement that Evergreen has ever bagged in their illustrious history. The revenue (to put a number) from phase 1 is expected to account for one tenth of Evergreen’s top line the duration of the project. In light of this, they have made a strategic decision to create a separate business vertical for this initiative with dedicated resources and a highly skilled team.

They have deployed their most skilled and experienced people to work on this project and Sanjit Kumar has been identified as the person who will lead this team. Sanjit has been hand-picked by the leadership because of his potential and consistent high performance over the years.

Group Discussion

  • Introduction
  • Additional information

You are Shashi, a Regional after-sales service manager at NextGen Motors, a leading company in the two-wheeler electric vehicle industry in India. After-sales service and customer satisfaction have been one of the distinguishing factors of NextGen Motors.

In the last few years, the organization has been looking to grow its business beyond its current markets. It expanded to the Southeast Asian market in countries like Philippines, Myanmar, Vietnam, Singapore, Malaysia, Thailand, and Indonesia 2 years ago after reviewing the market conditions. Currently NextGen holds 6.5% of the market share in the entire region. Given the competitive landscape of the EV two-wheeler market and the time it takes for a new entrant to establish itself, NextGen has secured a significant market share.

In the next 3 years, NextGen’s focus is to increase the market presence to 11% in these countries. Since the focus has been mostly to make its presence in the urban areas, it feels that there is scope to explore its potential in the rural areas now. To support this, after-sales strategy also needs to grow alongside sales to cater to the growth.

NextGen’s commitment to innovation and customer satisfaction has helped build a loyal customer base. Additionally, the company also has a dedicated team based out of India providing specialized services which require expertise in special areas.

Now, with its focus on expanding market share, the organization has called in for a strategy meeting.

Key Discussion

The Regional Heads of NextGen are invited to a strategy meeting discussion.  You along with other Regional Heads are expected to plan and strategize on how NextGen can expand its after-sales service network profitably in the Southeast Asian market. The meeting duration is 40 minutes, and you have 30 minutes to prepare for the discussion, with the following agenda:

  • Formulate an after-sales strategy aligned with the sales strategy to increase the market share from 6.5% to 11%.
  • Establish a set of performance indicators to evaluate the effectiveness of the expansion strategy focusing on customer satisfaction.
  • Create an implementation plan to meet the above objectives across the short and long term. 

Points to Consider

  • Assess the unique challenges and opportunities present in the Southeast Asian after-sales service sector that NextGen may encounter.
  • Utilize advanced technology to enhance the efficiency of after-sales service operations and ensure a smooth customer experience.
  • Maintain uniformity in service quality across all countries/regions to uphold NextGen's standards.

In the Southeast Asian market, local service centers are crucial, especially where authorized centers are unavailable. Major cities have well-equipped service centers with trained professionals, while rural areas face challenges with fewer, less equipped centers, limited spare parts, low connectivity, and high service times.

Local Regulations, Laws, and other challenges

Challenges

Description

Import Taxes and Duties

Countries like Malaysia, Indonesia and Vietnam have seen an increase in import duties and taxes over the past few years, raising concerns on the cost implications associated with imported 2 wheelers. These markets, being controlled by few players in the market makes it difficult for NextGen to put competitive pricing strategies.

Diverse regulations and emission standards

When it comes to local regulations and policies, different countries in South East Asia have varied policies and regulations that must be complied with when it comes to vehicle standards (safety, emission). Hence, a blanket approach cannot be taken when it comes to products and services complying with local standards and policies

Supply Chain of spare parts

Since NextGen is a new entrant in the market, its network of spare parts across these countries is still at a very nascent stage. Any plan to expand after-sales service should include a plan to strengthen the network of spare parts supply and distribution across these countries.

Talent Landscape

VoltWheels and EcoRide Motors dominate the local market, making them preferred employers due to their strong presence and community ties. Currently, NextGen's local Talent Acquisition team is working hard to build robust sales teams. The after-sales service team consists of 1,000 employees in India coordinating with 200 regional employees. However, this model is unsustainable for NextGen's expansion plans. Therefore, an effective talent acquisition strategy is essential for both sales and after-sales teams in the region.

Market Duopoly

Since the South East Asian market is controlled by 2 major players (VoltWheels and EcoRide), it becomes difficult for NextGen to expand aggressively, be it sales or after-sales. However, if NextGen doesn’t expand, the 2 giants will eat into NextGen’s market share in the near future.

After-sales customer preferences (Aggregated across the different countries)

Customer preferences in terms of after-sales in southeast Asia is diverse due to the region’s varying economic, cultural, and demographical landscapes. Customers generally prefer easily accessible service centers with quick turnaround time. An option to book and track services through smartphone application is highly appreciated. Additionally, importance is given to high quality services with competitive costing.

    You are responsible for complete product development cycle including market research, bench marking, project definition, concept design, prototype development, testing and validation, meeting the deadlines leading upto mass production, meeting budget requirements and target for the project defined by the company in advance.

    You have immense experience in identification and solving of various production and service complaints during new launch, preparing design guidelines and DFMEA of the electric power train parts and vehicle components.

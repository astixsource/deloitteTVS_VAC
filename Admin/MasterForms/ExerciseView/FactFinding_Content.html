This exercise helps to determine your ability to research information, to make decisions and present those decisions. Following these instructions is a short description of a situation demanding your immediate decision.

To enable you to reach your decision, a Resource Person will answer any questions you wish to ask about the situation. The Resource Person has considerable amount of information, which you can obtain by asking specific questions. If you ask general questions, you will be advised to make them more specific.

The Resource Person is an impartial outsider in this exercise and represents no one in the organisation.

You will firstly read ‘The Situation’ and prepare questions. Then you will ask questions, to consider the information and to arrive at your decision. You will then be asked to present your decision. The Resource Person will then question you on your decision.

You are Shashi, the Regional Service Manager at NextGen Motors, responsible for ensuring smooth service operations and customer satisfaction across your region.

Time:

10 minutes Read the brief and prepare your questions.

10 minutes Interview the Resource Person to obtain the information you require to enable you to make the decision.

5 mins:  The last 5 mins will be used to present your decision and the reasoning behind it to the resource person.

NextGen has faced a 35% increase in customer complaints over the last six months, causing delays in vehicle servicing. This rise in complaints coincides with the introduction of a new model variant one year ago. An external consultant was hired to investigate and provide recommendations. While some corrective actions have been implemented of the recommendations made, others require careful consideration due to the associated risks. You are responsible for reviewing the consultant's recommendations to decide on the best course of action.

The project is related to Vehicle-to-vehicle communication (V2V). Put simply, this technology will allow vehicles on the road to communicate with each other wirelessly in case of an emergency. For this, two-wheelers will need to be equipped with sensors to measure speed, braking, location, etc. Vehicle-to-vehicle communication’s ability to wirelessly exchange information about the speed and position of surrounding vehicles will help avoid crashes, ease traffic congestion, and improve the environment.
